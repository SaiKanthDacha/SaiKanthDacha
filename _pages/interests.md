### Biking

I am passionate about endurance biking. I have taken part in a number of 100km+ rides at IIT Madras and Chennai as part of various cycling clubs at the institute and city levels, usually on my Rockrider 5.0 (it was a mountain/hybrid bike). After moving to the US, I purchased the 2017 model of the Kona Jake Cyclocross. Although I haven't been able to ride as actively as I did back at IIT, I have nevertheless gone on a bunch of solo long rides here in the DC area. My favorites routes for longer rides include [Baltimore and back](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.strava.com%2Factivities%2F1052379983&sa=D&sntz=1&usg=AFQjCNFnHgJcPirKbAIpnOBrmMBwL1uVaQ) and also [Annapolis and back](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.strava.com%2Factivities%2F1240982140&sa=D&sntz=1&usg=AFQjCNEDxFiumgHOV0bKGolaWtboLOfmtg). Baltimore and Annapolis are both great places to visit, and I highly recommend the trails and roads leading to these cities from Washington, D.C.

### Chess

From the original Indian version of the game "chaturanga" to becoming a benchmark for artificial intelligence, chess has survived about 1500 years through periods of time where humanity has witnessed immense change. In the history of humanity, there has perhaps never been a more iconic game. Cultural references to this great game are just about everywhere -- movies, TV shows, poetry, books and even modern geopolitics. Go and Shogi are perhaps the only other such examples, but the widespread popularity of Chess all across the planet -- in the age of incredible computer games of all genres -- is something that I find fascinating. I only started playing a couple of years ago, so I am no Master. But if you are someone that enjoys the game, feel free to hit me up on [Lichess](https://lichess.org/@/saikanthdacha)!
