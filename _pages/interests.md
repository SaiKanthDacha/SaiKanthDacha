### Photography

I am an avid photographer. I own the Canon EOS 1200D DSLR with the 18-55 mm and 55-250 mm lenses. The fascinatingly beautiful campus that IIT Madras was, it proved to be paradise for the wildlife photographer and nature lover in me. I maintain an online portfolio of my pictures on [500px](https://www.500px.com/saikanthdacha) and [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/saikanthdacha).

### Writing

Lately, I have taken to writing down my thoughts on issues that interest me. I find that writing helps me develop better clarity in my thoughts and opinions, and helps me think more critically. As my PhD supervisor would probably agree, I have some ways to go when it comes to writing well. But I hope to sustain this habit and improve. You can find my writing here on [Medium](https://www.medium.com/@saikanthdacha).

### Biking

I am passionate about endurance biking. I have taken part in a number of 100km+ rides at IIT Madras and Chennai as part of various cycling clubs at the institute and city levels. Back there, I rode the Rockrider 5.0 mountain bike. After moving to the US, I purchased the 2017 model of the Kona Jake Cyclocross. Although I have not been able to ride as actively as I did back at IIT, I have nevertheless gone on a bunch of solo long rides here in the DC area. My favorites include the [ride to Baltimore and back](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.strava.com%2Factivities%2F1052379983&sa=D&sntz=1&usg=AFQjCNFnHgJcPirKbAIpnOBrmMBwL1uVaQ), and the one to [Annapolis and back](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.strava.com%2Factivities%2F1240982140&sa=D&sntz=1&usg=AFQjCNEDxFiumgHOV0bKGolaWtboLOfmtg). They are both great places to visit, and I highly recommend the trails and roads leading to these places from DC!

### Reading

I enjoy reading, be it over a cup of coffee or when I am traveling. For the past year or two, I have been primarily reading non-fiction. I am currently reading 'Good Economics for Hard Times', written by Nobel Prize winners Abhijit Banerjee and Esther Duflo. Given my recent interest in economics, another book I have lined up right after this is Raghuram Rajan's latest 'The Third Pillar'. If physics doesn't work out for me, I would enjoy working in economics! When not reading, I enjoy spending that time instead on nerdy YouTube channels and podcasts, unless of course I decide to binge watch a TV show!

### Chess

From the Indian version of the game "chaturanga" to becoming a benchmark test for artificial intelligence developers, chess has survived for thousands of years, through periods of time where humanity saw immense change. I only started playing a couple of years ago. There are few things more satisfying than a well-played game of chess.
