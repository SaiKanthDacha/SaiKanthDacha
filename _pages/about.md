My name is Sai Kanth, I am a graduate student at the [University of Maryland](www.umd.edu). I am currently pursuing my PhD in Physics. In 2016, I graduated from the [Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras](www.iitm.ac.in) with a Bachelor of Technology (B. Tech) degree in Engineering Physics.

My research interests lie in the broad area of photonics. Optical communication, nonlinear optics and multimode fiber optics are of specific interest to me. Through my work during my bachelor's degree, my summer research at Caltech's LIGO Laboratory and my current doctoral research, I have discovered my interest in the applications of photonics in modern day technologies and scientific experiments, from subsea fiber-optical communication to gravitational wave detectors. The potential to use of light in novel ways to improve our understanding of the universe or to push the limits of technology is what inspires me.

## Current Research

I am currently working with Prof. Thomas E. Murphy as part of the [Photonics Research Laboratory](www.photonics.umd.edu) at the University of Maryland. My current work is on experimental investigation of nonlinear optics in multimode fibers. Despite having been around since before the advent of the single mode fiber, nonlinear optics in multimode fibers remains, to this day, relatively understudied. Over the past few years, however, there has been a revival of interest in understanding nonlinear optics in these systems. Currently, my work is focused on developing and employing high-resolution spatiotemporal characterization tools to better understand the physics governing intermodal nonlinearity.
